<h2><strong>Understanding Resistance Ratios</strong></h2>
<p>Standard pulley systems operate on a 1 to 1 resistance ratio, meaning the weight loaded onto the stack directly matches the resistance a patient experiences during exercise. This straightforward mechanic makes dosage calculation simple and accurate, which matters considerably for early stage rehabilitation protocols requiring precise, controlled loading.</p>
<h2><strong>Weight Stack Options to Consider</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li>30 pound stacks suit early stage and gentle rehabilitation work</li>
<li>50 pound stacks support general mid stage strengthening protocols</li>
<li>70 pound stacks accommodate more advanced strengthening as patients progress</li>
</ul>
<p>Choosing the right weight stack depends heavily on your typical patient population. Clinics treating mostly post surgical patients may lean toward lighter options, while general strengthening focused practices might prioritize the higher end of the range.</p>
<h4><strong>Should You Buy Multiple Weight Options?</strong></h4>
<p>Some clinics purchase multiple units across different weight ranges to cover their entire patient population without needing to swap plates constantly. Others choose one versatile stack size and supplement with other equipment for outlier cases.</p>
<h2><strong>Physical Dimensions and Space Planning</strong></h2>

Confirming these dimensions against your available wall space before ordering helps avoid installation surprises.</p>
